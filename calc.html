How Simple Interest Works
The Formula
Total Interest = P × R × T
P = principal · R = rate · T = years

Each period: Interest = P × R ÷ Periods/yr

The interest amount is identical every period — always based on the original principal.
vs. Amortizing
Amortizing charges interest on the remaining balance — so interest shrinks each period as you pay down principal. Early payments are mostly interest; later payments are mostly principal.

Simple interest generally costs more on short-term loans.
Common Uses
• Private & hard money loans
• Bridge loans
• Land contracts & seller financing
• Most US auto loans
• Personal loans from individuals

Always confirm the method with your lender.
